# Learnings for the intraday S&P 500 reversal strategy.

[ALWAYS]
- The first 30 minutes after the open (09:30–10:00 ET) are high-noise chop — never
  recommend GO during this window regardless of signal count.
- Macro-driven selloffs (CPI, PPI, Fed, geopolitical shocks) follow a typical pattern:
  open down → morning selloff → exhaustion bottom (often 11:00–11:30 ET) → recovery.
  This is the highest-probability setup for this strategy.
- A TICK reading below -1000 followed by a snap above +200 is a stronger flush signal
  than a -800 to +100 reading — weight accordingly.
- On trend days (market making lower lows throughout the session with no bounces), this
  strategy does not apply — return GO: false and explain it is a trend day, not a
  mean-reversion day.
- If the VIX is making new session highs at the time of the signal, panic is not
  exhausted — return GO: false.
- 11:00–11:30 ET is classically where institutional algorithms end morning positioning.
  Setups that trigger near this window have higher historical success rates.
- A 5-minute candle that closes ABOVE the high of the lowest candle (the "hammer bar")
  is the structural confirmation — without this, the candle structure condition is not met.

[PERISCOPE_SCREENSHOT]
- When a Periscope screenshot is provided, look for:
  * Large green GEX bars at or near the current price — these act as dealer-bid support
  * The gamma flip level — above it dealers amplify moves up; below it they amplify down
  * Net MM positioning switching from net short gamma to net long gamma at the low
- If the screenshot is unclear, blurry, or cropped such that the key levels are not
  visible, state that in REASONING and base the verdict on the numeric signals only.
- Periscope shows data in 10-minute buckets — confirm the screenshot timestamp aligns
  with the signal time before drawing conclusions.

[OPENING_GAP]
- The signal summary includes `gap=X.XX%` — the difference between today's RTH open and
  the prior session close.
- Large negative gap (gap < -0.5%): primary target setup. A macro-driven selloff from an
  already-depressed open is the highest-probability mean-reversion scenario. Weight signal
  confluence heavily and proceed if conditions are met.
- Small gap (-0.5% to +0.5%): quiet support bounce setup. Valid, but price has less
  downside momentum to reverse — require cleaner GEX floor confirmation (clear large green
  bar at or below current price) and preference for 4+ conditions before going positive.
- Large positive gap (gap > +0.5%): price opened near the session high. This strategy does
  not target long entries from the high of the day. Return GO: false unless there has been
  a significant intraday selloff that has reset price back to a GEX support level — in
  that case treat it as a quiet bounce setup and apply the higher confirmation bar above.

[ES_FUTURES]
- The signal summary includes `ES=XXXX.XX` — the front-month E-mini S&P 500 futures price
  (denominated in SPX points, roughly 10× SPY price).
- If ES is already recovering and leading SPY higher while SPY sits at a support level,
  that is a bullish divergence — additional confirmation the floor is holding.
- If ES is still making new lows at the time of the signal, the support level in SPY may
  not hold — weight this against the entry and reduce confidence accordingly.
- Pre-market: a large ES discount to prior SPY close confirms the gap-down setup before
  RTH opens.

[RISK]
- Never recommend a stop more than 0.5% below entry — the strategy targets tight
  risk with asymmetric reward.
- The default target is the prior session close or the opening price, whichever is
  closer. Do not project targets beyond that level.
